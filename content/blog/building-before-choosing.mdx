The three doors in front of me are quant finance, academic research, and
startups. Everyone's advice is to pick early and compound. The problem is
that from the outside, all three are indistinguishable marketing.

So instead of choosing, I have been running the experiment directly: a
trading-competition strategy and an execution model for the quant door, a
neural-quantum-states project pushed toward an actual publishable question for
the research door, and products with real users for the startup door. Each one
generates data no amount of career-advice reading could.

What the data says so far: the common factor across everything I enjoy is
*building instruments* — things that let you see something you could not see
before. A spectrometer is an instrument. A VMC sampler is an instrument. A
lead–lag detector is an instrument. Which industry I end up in matters less
than whether the work is instrument-building.

That reframing is worth more to me than any of the three labels. The doors
stay open; the notebook fills up either way.
