The goal: reproduce the core result of Carleo & Troyer (2017) with nothing but
NumPy, and then push past it with an entanglement-based diagnostic.

## The ansatz

The wavefunction is a restricted Boltzmann machine with the hidden units traced
out analytically:

$$
\psi_\theta(\sigma) = e^{\sum_i a_i \sigma_i} \prod_{j=1}^{M} 2\cosh\!\Big(b_j + \sum_i W_{ij}\,\sigma_i\Big)
$$

The parameters $\theta = (a, b, W)$ are complex. For $N$ spins and $M = \alpha N$
hidden units, the ansatz has $O(\alpha N^2)$ parameters — polynomial, while the
Hilbert space is $2^N$. That gap is the entire bet of the method.

## Sampling

Expectation values come from Metropolis–Hastings over spin configurations,
with single spin flips as proposals. The acceptance ratio only needs
amplitude ratios, never normalization:

```python
def log_psi(sigma, a, b, W):
    theta = b + sigma @ W
    return sigma @ a + np.sum(np.log(2 * np.cosh(theta)))

def metropolis_step(sigma, a, b, W, rng):
    i = rng.integers(len(sigma))
    proposal = sigma.copy()
    proposal[i] *= -1
    log_ratio = 2 * np.real(log_psi(proposal, a, b, W) - log_psi(sigma, a, b, W))
    if np.log(rng.random()) < log_ratio:
        return proposal
    return sigma
```

## Local energy

For the TFIM Hamiltonian
$H = -J\sum_i \sigma^z_i \sigma^z_{i+1} - h \sum_i \sigma^x_i$,
the local energy at configuration $\sigma$ is

$$
E_\mathrm{loc}(\sigma) = -J\sum_i \sigma_i \sigma_{i+1} \; - \; h \sum_i \frac{\psi(\sigma^{(i)})}{\psi(\sigma)}
$$

where $\sigma^{(i)}$ is $\sigma$ with spin $i$ flipped. The off-diagonal term is
where the wavefunction ratio trick earns its keep — one flipped spin only
changes one row of the $\cosh$ products, so the ratio is $O(M)$, not $O(NM)$.

## The diagnostic I actually care about

Everyone plots energy convergence. The more interesting question: does the
network *learn entanglement in the right order*? Track the second Rényi
entropy of a half-chain cut during training,

$$
S_2 = -\ln \operatorname{Tr}\rho_A^2,
$$

estimated with the swap trick on two replica chains. Near the critical point
$h/J = 1$, $S_2$ should grow logarithmically with subsystem size — and my
working hypothesis is that the *trajectory* of $S_2$ during optimization says
something about when the ansatz is under-parameterized, before the energy
error tells you.
